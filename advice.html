Here, we have some tips on how to achieve sustainability outside your daily actions:

First, joining an organization is always a great way to help out!
If you want to look into various organizations near you, click here for more information.

Also, vote with your money! Try to avoid shopping from companies you know abuse the environment.
To learn the environmental impact of a company, click here.

It's always good to eat local, both to support your community and to promote small farming over huge corporations.

Try to become aware of how much plastic you use each day, and where you can cut back.
To calculate how much plastic you consume, use this calculator!
And to see another representation of your impact, click calculator!

Unplug your devices after using them. Even when your computer is sleeping, it still eats up energy!

Use bamboo toothbrushes, cutlery, etc.

Lower the temperature on your AC by four degrees and turn on your fan.
The wind chill will have the same effect as those four degrees!

Educate on how politicians are approaching the climate crisis
For information on the Green New Deal, click here.
    (If you're slightly more ambitious and want to read the actual Green New Deal, click here.)
For information on the climate debate, click here.

Try to use mass transit or bikes instead of cars.
If you absolutely must drive, try to carpool with others.

Airdrying your laundry (instead of using the machine) cuts down on energy consumption
